# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Marcus "Moneybags" Calloway
**Nickname/Handle:** @BudgetBackpackerBoss
**Age:** 28
**Nationality:** American
**Occupation:** Self-proclaimed "Travel Finance Guru" (unemployed trust fund kid)

## Archetype
**Primary:** Budget Traveler (who flaunts wealth)

**Secondary traits:** Social media obsessed, financially reckless, manipulative

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Greed

**How it manifests:**
- Always talking about money and "smart investments"
- Lends money with high interest to fellow travelers
- Judges people by their financial status
- Hoards cash but flaunts expensive gear

**How it kills them:**
His obsession with money and distrust of others leads him to refuse a desperate request for $200, triggering a violent reaction from someone he's been financially manipulating.

- **Height/Build:** 5'11", athletic but slightly soft from hostel life
- **Hair:** Perfectly styled blonde hair, always with sunglasses perched on head
- **Distinguishing features:** Expensive smartwatch on one wrist, cheap plastic bracelets from "local artisans" on the other
- **Typical outfit:** Patagonia vest over a cheap tank top, designer hiking shoes, cargo shorts with too many pockets

### Fears
- Surface fear: Losing his "investment capital"

## Background

### Origin Story
Marcus grew up in affluent Connecticut, the son of a hedge fund manager. Despite having a trust fund, he's obsessed with proving he can "make it on his own" through travel. His "budget backpacking" is a performance - he stays in cheap hostels but always has expensive gear and talks about his "investment portfolio."

### Why They Travel
He's running from the expectation to join the family business and trying to prove he's "street smart." In reality, he's just looking for easy ways to make money off fellow travelers.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
After getting kicked out of a luxury hostel for running an illegal money-lending scheme, Marcus ended up at The Wanderer's Nest in Bangkok, where he sees fresh opportunities to "help" financially struggling backpackers.
